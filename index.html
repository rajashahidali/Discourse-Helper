About Discourse Helper

What Is Discourse Analysis?

Discourse analysis is the study of language in use — examining how meaning is constructed not just in individual sentences but across entire texts, conversations, and social contexts. It investigates how language reflects and shapes power, identity, ideology, and social relations.

From a political speech to a social media post, from a job interview to a news headline, discourse analysis reveals the hidden structures that govern communication and shape how we understand the world.

How AI Assists Learning

Large language models have been trained on vast linguistic corpora, making them remarkably capable discourse partners. Discourse Helper applies AI to provide instant, academically-grounded analysis guided by established frameworks from Halliday, Fairclough, Van Dijk, Sacks, Grice, and others.

The AI does not replace critical thinking — it scaffolds it. By providing initial analysis, flagging key features, and explaining theoretical concepts, Discourse Helper helps users develop their own analytical skills more rapidly.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What types of texts can Discourse Helper analyze? ▾
Any written text: news articles, academic papers, speeches, social media posts, advertisements, interviews, political documents, literary texts, and conversational transcripts.
Is the AI analysis academically reliable? ▾
The AI provides analysis grounded in established linguistic frameworks and is a valuable starting point. However, all academic work should be verified against primary sources and peer-reviewed literature. Use it as a scaffold, not a final authority.
Can I use it for my university assignments? ▾
Yes, as a research and learning tool. Always disclose AI use according to your institution's academic integrity policy, and ensure all submitted work reflects your own analysis and understanding.
Which discourse analysis frameworks are supported? ▾
Hallidayan SFL, Fairclough's CDA, Van Dijk's socio-cognitive CDA, Wodak's Discourse-Historical Approach, Gricean Pragmatics, Relevance Theory, Conversation Analysis (Sacks/Schegloff), and Framing Analysis.
How do the 10 analysis workflows work? ▾
Each workflow opens the chatbot pre-loaded with a specialized system prompt and starter message for that analysis type. You paste your text and get a structured analysis focused on that framework.
